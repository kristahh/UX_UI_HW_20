FUREVER FREED
DOG RESCUE
WEBSITE REDESIGN

OVERVIEW

A non-profit organization desperately needed a redesign to better share the information + resources their users seek and to convey a sense of credibility that their 13 years of experience provides.

GOAL

Redesign the existing non-profit website to make it appear more up to date and more user friendly. Solve the issues identified by users + stakeholder.

Andrea, a Stakeholder at Furever Freed shared that the organization has been around for 13 years, and the website is showing its age. It is: out of date, not user friendly, and they receive a lot of messages regarding the issues people are having. She stated her concern as:

“We could be missing out on really good adopters due to technical issues with the site”

To further understand the content and the expectations of a dog rescue website, we did some usability testing of the current site. The people we tested had a lot of strong opinions about the site and pointed out some glaring pain points, echoing details of our stakeholder highlighted, but also stated that:

“The initial credibility of a site is a judgement based a lot on how it looks.”

User interviews were conducted to discover some of the deeper thoughts and feelings pertaining to the dog rescue process. Interviewees had all previously adopted rescue dogs themselves, and one had also worked at a dog rescue.. The greatest insight was that they unanimously believe:

“The process needn't be easy, but accurate information and clarity of process is most important.”

Someone looking to rescue a dog seeks the joy that having a dog brings to your life. They believe the process doesn't necessarily need to be easy however, it does need to provide clear information on how to proceed, and also,
convey a sense of credibility.

  • Wants a clear and comprehensive presentation of information
  • Prefers a clear and thoughtful application process, as opposed to quick and easy.
  • Needs to have confidence that the dog she will adopt is a good fit for her and her family.

Olivia Smith
Age: 45
Lives in: Port Moody BC
Family: Married, mother of 2
Profession: Kinesiologist

  • “I want to feel like a website is credible and safe to share my personal information with”
  • “I want to see and know about the dogs that need to be adopted… I love the photos and videos!”
  • “Rescuing my dog was the best decision I ever made”

How might we present the information to prospective dog owners in a way that makes them feel both informed and trusting of the site, so that they may proceed with the adoption process both comfortably and confidently?

Include a self awareness questionnaire as part of the “How to Adopt” process

Create a considered online fillable application form.

Create a template for adoptable dog profiles, and filters for searching.

Present information in a clear and easy to find /read / digest format.

TESTING THROUGH ITERATIONS:

Taking the time to do usability testing at different stages of design, assured us that the new iteration was working to satisfy the needs of the user, to discover if anything was missing, and simply to determine preferences.

We used rapid A/B style testing when we had variations of a design and wanted quick feedback on which to proceed with.

A usability test discovered that after completing the new online application form, having a confirmation page would assure the applicant everything worked as it should.

The “How to Help” page went through several iterations, before arriving at the full colour, hi-fidelity prototype version.

NEXT STEPS:

Present current iteration to our stakeholder for further feedback: implement any necessary changes.
Keep iterating and improving until our design resonates with our client.
Continue usability testing across platforms to ensure that users like Olivia are finding what they need.

FINAL THOUGHTS

The information collected though our interview participants acted as guiding principles throughout the process and ultimately improved our final resolutions. We learned the most + received inspiration from the stakeholder interview, user interviews and usability testing. We identified the specific features our client+users both want/need us to develop: FAQ section, adoptable dogs template, a self reflection questionnaire, fillable application, etc.

The process of UX/UI design is enhanced and improved through multiple iterations. We elaborated and evolved the flows, functions, components, and design, which is a constant cycle in the design process. The current design solves for the technical issues outlined by our research and interviews, but also imbues the feelings of warmth, kindness and trustworthiness that we sought to convey.
